PM Best Practices with AI
01 · Context first
Always anchor prompts with context
Start every prompt with your product domain, user segment, and stage (discovery, design, GTM). AI output quality scales linearly with context richness.
02 · Persona lock
Assign a specific expert role
Prefix prompts with "Act as a senior PM at a Series B SaaS company…" to get outputs calibrated to your business context, not generic advice.
03 · Output format
Request structured deliverables
Specify output format explicitly — "Return a table with columns: Story, Acceptance Criteria, Priority, and Effort." This eliminates back-and-forth editing.
04 · Iterative drafts
Treat AI output as a first draft
Use AI to generate v0.1, then refine with domain knowledge. The goal is 70% time saved on structure — you own the strategy and nuance.
05 · Constraint injection
Add real-world constraints
Include constraints like "team of 4 engineers, 6-week sprint, existing React codebase" so AI solutions are implementable, not theoretical.
06 · Devil's advocate
Ask AI to challenge your plan
Append "Now list 5 reasons this plan could fail and how to mitigate each" to any PRD or roadmap prompt for built-in red-teaming.
07 · Stakeholder lens
Reframe outputs per audience
Run the same PRD through: "Summarise this for an engineer", "for a CFO", and "for a new customer". Saves hours of reformatting.
08 · Version control
Document prompt + output pairs
Save high-performing prompts in a shared Notion page. When a prompt produces great output, it becomes a team asset — not a personal shortcut.
AI Usage Transparency Guidelines
Disclose AI-generated artefacts in reviews
Label any PRD, story, or spec that was drafted with AI assistance so stakeholders can calibrate how much editorial judgement was applied before the review.
Never ship AI output without human validation
AI-generated acceptance criteria, edge cases, and metrics must be reviewed by a PM who understands the customer context before being added to the backlog.
Identify and document model limitations per use case
Note where AI was unreliable for your context (e.g. niche regulatory domains, non-English user research) and add a manual review step in your team's workflow.
Do not input confidential customer data into public LLMs
Use anonymised or synthetic data when prompting. Work with your legal and security teams to define what data is safe to include in prompts on approved tools.
Communicate AI use to users where it impacts their experience
If your product uses AI to generate recommendations, summaries, or decisions that affect users, include clear disclosure in the UI and your privacy policy.
Track AI leverage metrics as a team health signal
Monitor the ratio of AI-assisted to fully manual artefacts each sprint. Aim for consistent use patterns — neither avoidance nor over-reliance — across the team.
